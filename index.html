INTRODUCTION: SOLAR PANEL

A solar panel is an assembly of photovoltaic solar cells mounted in a frame(usually rectangular). Solar panels capture sunlight as a source of radiant energy in the form of direct current (D.C) electricity.

MATERIALS NEEDED

  • Solar Panel with hydraulic legs
  • Wipers
  • Water sprinker
  • Heat Sensor
  • Motor

PROBLEMS OF THE EXISTING SOLAR PANEL

  • The accumulation of dust on the panel surface in the absence of rainfall.
  • Animal droppings staining the panel surface.
  • Water droplets on the panel takes a longer time to dry up.

OUR PROPOSAL ON THE MODIFICATION OF SOLAR PANELS

Our project is to design a cleaning system for the solar PV panels during dry season. Our project is equipped with a sprinkler and a wiper to remove the accumulated dust and other dirt from solar panels surface. The proposed cleaning system can work for a long time efficiently. Therefore, our project work can be considering as a promising and efficient system to solve the problem of poor performance of the photovoltaic cells in areas that experience dusty environment and external pollutants. In addition, our project also has a sensitive rotatory feature that inclines the panel for maximum absorption of sun rays.

SOLAR PANEL MECHANISM

HOW SOLAR PANEL WORKS

When the sun shines onto a solar panel, energy from the sunlight is absorbed by the PV cells in the panel. This energy creates electrical charges that move in response to an internal electrical field in the cell, causing electricity to flow.

PARTS OF THE SOLAR PANEL

  1. The Solar PV cells convert sunlight directly into DC electrical energy.
  2. The glass sheet protects the PV cells from the weather and impact from hail or airborne debris.
  3. The aluminium frame plays a critical role by both protecting the edge of the laminate section housing the cells and providing a solid structure to mount the solar panel in position.
  4. The Encapsulant is used to encapsulate the cells and hold them in position during manufacture.
  5. The backsheet is the rear most layer of common solar panels which as acts as a moisture barrier and final external skin to provide both mechanical protection and electrical insulation.

  7. The junction box is needed to securely attach the cables required to interconnect the panels.

SOLAR PANEL INSTALLATION PROCESS

  1. Set up scaffolding to ensure safety during the installation
  2. Install the solar panel mounting system to an angle between 18 to 36 degrees for maximum sunlight exposure
  3. Install the solar panels on the mounting structure.
  4. The next step in the installation process is to install the electrical wiring. In most cases,  MC4 connectors are used because they are suited for all types of solar panels. Make sure to  shut off the household’s electricity supply during the wiring installation.
  5. After that, the solar inverter must be connected to the system. It is typically installed near the main panel and it could be both indoors and outdoors.
  6. Thereafter, the solar inverter has to be connected to the solar battery.
  7. The next step is to make sure that the  inverter is connected to the consumer unit to generate electricity. A generation meter should also be connected to monitor the amount of electricity the solar panels actually produce.
  8. The final step is to switch the power on and test the newly installed solar panel system.
